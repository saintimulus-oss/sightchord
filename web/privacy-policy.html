Chordest

Privacy Policy

Chordest is designed as a local-first music practice app. This policy covers the Android, iOS, and web builds published by Saintimulus OSS.

Support Terms of Use Open App

Last updated: March 21, 2026

What Chordest collects

The current Chordest mobile release does not require account creation and does not intentionally collect personal information from inside the app.

The app stores practice settings, notation preferences, and learning progress locally on the device so your sessions can resume with the same configuration later.

What Chordest does not currently use

  • No in-app ads.
  • No in-app analytics or crash-reporting SDKs in the current repository build.
  • No account login, profile system, or cloud sync in the current repository build.
  • No camera, microphone, location, contacts, or photo-library access in the current repository build.

How data is stored

Practice progress and app settings are stored locally on your device through platform preference storage. If you uninstall the app, clear app data, or move to a new device, that local data may be lost unless a future release adds sync or export features.

Third-party platforms

If you download Chordest from Google Play or the App Store, those platforms may process technical or account information under their own privacy policies. If you access the public website, GitHub Pages and related infrastructure may process standard web request logs under GitHub's policies.

Children's privacy

Chordest is a general-audience music education app. It is not directed to children under 13 as a standalone child-focused service, and the current release does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

If a future release adds analytics, accounts, cloud sync, purchases, or any new data use, this policy will be updated before or alongside that release.

Contact

For support requests, bug reports, or privacy questions, use the support page at support.html.
